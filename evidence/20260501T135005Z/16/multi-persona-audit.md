Verdicts and signed findings recorded below for the next session to act on.

## Top blocking issues (consensus across personas)

1. **No named protagonist.** Lucia in Madrid (storyteller's suggestion)
   should open Section 1, walk through Sections 5, 15 and 17, and be
   the person whose Tuesday match the reality-check tests are protecting.
2. **Two simultaneous beachheads (Spain + Russia).** CPO and Strategist
   both flag this. Pick Spain only. Russia / CIS becomes a Phase-2
   contingent on the Spanish funnel hitting >40% direct-traffic share.
3. **Unit economics missing.** CPO calls out: at EUR 7.99/month with
   typical consumer-fitness churn (6–8%), LTV is ~EUR 90–120. A
   $150 community CAC is fine; a $500 organiser CAC monetised only
   via consumer subs is not. Add LTV, payback, gross margin, blended
   CAC by channel.
4. **TAM-SAM-SOM funnel mixes incompatible market definitions.**
   Strategist: the Deloitte EUR 4.015B is club real-estate revenue,
   not addressable software wallet. Replace TAM with derived figure
   (35M players × ~EUR 30/year blended digital spend × ~5% paid
   penetration ceiling).
5. **SOM math inconsistent.** Headline says 5,000–20,000; worked
   example produces 7,500 at EUR 720K — but Year-1 range is EUR
   0.48M–1.92M. Show the formula, single conversion driver, single
   ARPU, transparent deltas.
6. **USP tries three jobs at once.** Copywriter + CPO both flag.
   Tighten to: "A padel rating that travels with you — earned from
   your phone, accepted at every club and tournament." Move
   coach-renewal language to the coach segment value-prop.
7. **Data moat asserted on top of OSS baseline.** Strategist: more
   data yields diminishing returns once the model converges. Name a
   proprietary loop (coach-confirmed labels on ambiguous rallies)
   that competitors using the same OSS pipeline cannot replicate.
8. **Competitive table has gaps.** Strategist names: SwingVision,
   Track Padel, Pulse Padel, Rally, Sportlogiq, Stats Perform, Volt,
   TopSpin Padel app, WhatsApp/Telegram self-organised rating groups.
9. **Headline is generic.** Copywriter rewrites: "The Rating That
   Travels: A Padel AI Coach Brief" or "35 Million Players, Zero
   Real Ratings — A Padel AI Coach Brief".
10. **Reality-check #5 is missing.** CPO: add a paid-second-event
    falsifier — "One FIP-affiliated bracket runs end-to-end seeded
    by the rating AND the organiser commits to a paid second event
    within 30 days."

## Sections to add

- **Unit economics block** at end of § 9 (Pricing): LTV, monthly churn,
  gross margin, CAC by channel, CAC-payback in months, LTV:CAC ratio.
- **12-week phased roadmap** between § 12 (capability map) and § 13
  (reality-check tests): map the three first moves to weeks, gate each
  week on a measurable threshold.
- **Funding / runway / hiring trigger** between roadmap and risks: name
  what unlocks the first external capital line and what the first three
  hires beyond solo execution are.
- **Data / IP / legal posture** between risks and category contradiction:
  match-video consent flow, GDPR + 152-FZ data-processing posture,
  player-data ownership.
- **Failure-mode for FIP-launches-its-own-rating risk**: what is the
  fallback rating standard if FIP ships first?

  weekend partner accepts; fears paying EUR 60 for a coach assessment
  that expires when she leaves the club.
- **Carla** (Barcelona, academy coach): wants a one-page recap she can
  hand a student before the renewal call without writing notes after
  every lesson; fears losing a paying student to a rival who shows
  progress charts.

  seed themselves cleanly on Saturday morning; fears running a
  tournament where seeding is publicly wrong.
- **Dmitri** (Moscow, Russian-language padel reader): wants tactical
  recaps in his language before English creators arrive; fears match
  data leaving the country under 152-FZ.

keeps a live mental picture.
